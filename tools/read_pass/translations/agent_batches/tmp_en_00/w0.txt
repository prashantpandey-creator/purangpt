Annotation
Today yoga has become a global phenomenon, yet despite this an encounter with a realized yogi remains a rare piece of fortune. Paramahansa Yogananda's world-famous bestseller "Autobiography of a Yogi" introduced millions of people to the transformative power of Kriya Yoga, but the story of Kriya does not end there.
The new and captivating authorized biography of the Fifth Guru of Kriya Yoga, Shailendra Sharma — "The Awakener" — acquaints the reader with the extraordinary life path of a contemporary yogi who, in the twenty-first century, continues the ancient tradition of Mahavatar Babaji and Lahiri Mahasaya.
The book illumines the mysteries of Creation, Time, the Void, and the Alchemy of yoga in a language accessible to uninitiated readers. In contrast to the idealized portraits of yoga so often encountered in spiritual literature, this narrative discloses the true essence of samadhi and the path to immortality.

By introducing the reader to the timeless wisdom of a contemporary yogi, "The Awakener" inspires by challenging everyday perceptions of reality.

About the Book

The authorized biography of the Fifth Guru of Kriya Yoga, Shailendra Sharma, introduces the reader to the extraordinary life path of a contemporary realized yogi who, in the twenty-first century, continues the ancient tradition of Mahavatar Babaji and Lahiri Mahasaya. "The Awakener" illumines Shailendra Sharma's views on creation, the eternal sound, alchemy, the path to self-realization, and the methods of attaining immortality. The book will help today's seekers of truth grasp such concepts as the consciousness of Time and the Void, and learn of the mechanics of samadhi and the logic of karma.

Introduction

"Arjuna said: O Keshava, who is he, established perfectly in divine wisdom, fully absorbed in pure spiritual consciousness (samadhi)? How does he speak? How does he sit? How does he walk?"
— Bhagavad Gita 2.54

The yogis say: "Truth is one, but the paths leading to It are many." Echoing this saying, the heroes, geniuses, and saints of the twentieth century have left us the milestones of their discoveries of Truth. Yet many of these discoveries remain general abstractions in need of decipherment. The realities of the twenty-first century pose new challenges. The growing ecological, economic, political, and moral crises of modern society underscore the urgent need to answer existential questions: who are we, how does life arise, what is the ultimate purpose of dwelling in a physical body so severely limited in time? Is this birth accidental, or is it the inevitable result of previous deeds and tendencies, a planned segment of an endless cycle? And if, according to certain religious convictions, the continuous incarnations are the consequence of past karma, what can be done to break this cycle? Can it, and should it, even be brought to an end? At some point we all ask ourselves: does a soul-plan exist? Could it be that our bodies are only a by-product of Nature's endless play?

Those who strive for spiritual development pose still harder questions. Can individual consciousness be preserved after the death of the physical body? Does that guarantee the "salvation" of the soul as it continues its endless journey through Time? And if eternal life exists, who or what will help us in the transition from the earthly plane into the astral world? Will we be able to retain the memory of our present personality after death? Is it even worth preserving it in the next life-cycle, or letting it go and playing the new role handed to us by the great producer of this Creation — Lila?

Sometimes the answers are encoded in the questions: the old rarely fits organically into the new. Time itself is changing, and only special adaptive methods can help "old" souls to assimilate into the new world.
These fundamental questions remain unanswered even with the arrival of high technologies that strive to process eternal philosophical questions in order to digest and digitize them, eventually squeezing them into manageable algorithms.
But before the triumph of technology turns these ideas into mobile applications, there is still time for reflection. Conscious living acquires paramount importance as consciousness itself grows. Some understand that the awakening of the spirit can take place here and now, while we still possess this unique, fragile, and precious human form. The body is at once a limitation and an encoded infinity, a mortal spacesuit of flesh and a reflection of past karmas. Yes, it is a factory of new bodies for old and new souls, but first and foremost it is the refuge of the mind. Is it possible to learn to manage it beyond physical parameters and, most importantly, to update its software? The discovery of the power of consciousness and new evidence of its undisclosed potential attract those who seek to step out of the limiting matrix and experience the astonishing state of union with their own spirit — invisible, yet tangible.
