## The Identity Doctrine (how names work)

> Names and forms (e.g., Krishna, Arjuna, yajna, charity, penance) are not merely historical or literal entities but are symbols for inner yogic processes and states of consciousness. The One unmanifest Time appears as the many through manifestation, yet all manifestations (yoga stages, practices, advice) are essentially the same unmanifest Time. The identity of a thing is determined by its relationship to the inner yogic path: it is either a state of limited consciousness (Asat) or a state of Time-consciousness (Sat). The ultimate identity is the unmanifest Time itself, which is both immanent in the Void and transcendent.

## Cosmology (the ground of reality)

> Time (unmanifest Brahma) is the ultimate creator, imperishable, beyond the mind. It is the one and only truth. The creation is manifested by this imperishable Brahma. Time is immanent in the Void (the consciousness of the Void). The three stages of yoga, their practice, and advice are manifestations of the unmanifest Time. There is no detailed creation or dissolution cycle described beyond this; the focus is on the inner realization of Time as the ultimate truth, and the process of awakening dormant consciousness by rising above physical limitations. Death is seen as a transition to a greater fear if one is established in Asat, but for the realized, death leads to knowledge of Time.
